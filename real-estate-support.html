Property Management

  1. Execute Tenancy Agreements
  2. Collect payments related to rentals payment on behalf of the Landlord and deposit into Landlord’s account
  3. Check In / Check Out
  4. Take inventory note in case of furnished / semi-furnished apartments
  5. Manage Tenancy Contract and all Tenant queries/complaints related to the property
  6. Renewal of Tenancy Agreement with renewal notice issuance as per Law
  7. Maintain necessary records of all payment receipts, Tenant documents etc.
  8. Update Landlord on a regular basis
  9. Biannual property Inspection or as required
  10. Do the maintenance for any minor and major repairs
  11. Recommend to Landlord any major or minor repairs required in the property in writing and execute the maintenance with written approval from the Landlord. Minor repairs are related to day-to-day wear and tear. Major repairs are related to water heaters, water pumps (if applicable), air-conditioning units and ducts and major plumbing and electrical issues but not limited to servicing, repairs/ and replacements
  12. The Managing Agent will reserve a certain amount per property from rental amounts towards petty cash to pay the applicable maintenance provider on behalf of the Landlord
  13. Coordinate with internal and external Leasing Consultants for quick leasing
